European Rabbit

Oryctolagus cuniculus • Champion Jumper of the Green Meadows

🐰 Introduction

Social Herbivore Agile Leaper Tunnel Architect

The European Rabbit is a small, highly social mammal famous for its long ears, fluffy cotton-ball tail, and energetic hopping. Originally native to southwestern Europe, rabbits have adapted to live in meadows, fields, woodlands, and urban parks all over the world.

Rabbits are key players in their native ecosystems. By grazing on grass and herbs, they help keep plant growth balanced. They are also known for building large, interconnected underground tunnel systems where families live safely together.

👂 Super-Senses and Long Ears

A rabbit’s ears are not just for looking cute! They are precision-engineered survival tools that help the rabbit monitor its surroundings for any sign of movement.

Independent Movement: Rabbits can rotate their ears up to $270^\circ$ independently. This allows them to pinpoint the exact direction of tiny sounds without turning their heads.

Air Conditioning: Those long ears also act as a personal cooling system. Large blood vessels in the ears expand on hot days to release excess body heat, helping the rabbit stay comfortable in the summer sun.

Panoramic Vision: Because their eyes are positioned high on the sides of their heads, rabbits have a nearly $360^\circ$ view of their surroundings, allowing them to spot dangers from almost any angle!

🏠 Underground Cities: The Warren

Rabbits are brilliant digging engineers. When multiple rabbit families live in the same meadow, they dig an interconnected maze of tunnels called a warren.

Safe Haven: Warrens can be incredibly complex, extending several meters underground. They feature multiple exits for quick escapes and cozy nesting chambers lined with soft grass and fur to keep baby rabbits (called *kits* or *kittens*) warm and secure.

✨ Amazing Rabbit Trivia

🦷
Never-Ending Teeth: A rabbit’s front teeth never stop growing! To prevent them from getting too long, rabbits chew on tough plant fibers, wood, and bark to naturally wear them down.
🥳
The "Binky" Dance: When a rabbit is extremely happy and excited, it will perform an acrobatic leap into the air, twisting its body and kicking its feet. Animal scientists call this joyful dance a binky!
🥗
Picky Eaters: Rabbits are strict herbivores. They love to eat fresh grass, clover, wildflower shoots, and leafy green vegetables. They digest their food twice to absorb every single healthy vitamin!
